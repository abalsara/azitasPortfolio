Increasing User Retention Through a Smarter Onboarding Experience

Redesigning and expanding Viata's onboarding form to support customization and reduce drop-off
Position UX Design Internship at Viata
Sector Startup, Event Planning, Travel Tech
My Role Research, Wireframing, Interface Design, Development
Project Duration 4 weeks (May 2025)
BACKGROUND
Product
Viata is a Seattle-based travel tech startup that helps event organizers create custom, interactive event maps for their attendees. These maps showcase venues, hotels, local attractions, and points of interest — making it easier for attendees to navigate and explore the event area.
Challenge
My primary task was to redesign one of Viata's onboarding form, used by event organizers to input essential event details. This form powers the back end of the map-building process.

The redesign brief was loosely defined — “improve usability and aesthetics” — leaving the core problem framing up to me. I had to identify usability issues, propose improvements, and ensure the new design aligned with the product's evolving visual direction.
Constraints
As with many early-stage startup projects, several real-world limitations shaped the approach:

  • Tight Timelines: The onboarding redesign was part of a larger product release, meaning timelines were tight and scope had to remain focused.
  • Startup Realities: With limited team size, budget, and access to users, research and testing had to be creative and scrappy.
  • No Design Mentor / First UX Role This was my first professional design experience, and I navigated the process independently — framing the problem, conducting research, iterating designs, and making strategic decisions as I went
RESEARCH & IDEATION
Approach
Given limited time and access to users, I used a combination of direct and indirect research methods to understand usability issues and opportunities within the onboarding form:

  • Heuristic Evaluation: I conducted an internal audit using Nielsen's usability heuristics to assess issues in consistency, feedback, and navigation.
  • Stakeholder Conversations: My manager shared insights from past interviews with event organizers to surface user needs and pain points.
  • Comparative Research: I reviewed Growth.Design case studies on onboarding best practices to understand patterns that support clarity, engagement, and retention.
  • Session Replays: I analyzed recordings of users interacting with the form to identify moments of confusion, hesitation, and drop-off.
